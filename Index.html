How I Lead
Content Operating
Model

This is the system I bring to every content operation I lead. It's not just a workflow. It's a leadership framework that keeps teams aligned, output scalable, and results tied directly to business objectives. Whether managing one brand or twelve, this is how I run it.

1
Strategy

I audit audience data, platform trends, and business goals before setting a single content pillar. Strategy without data is just opinion. I don't guess, I build from evidence.

2
Plan

I build systems, not just calendars. I oversee and approve editorial frameworks, campaign briefs, and shot lists, creating structures that editors and videographers can execute consistently without bottlenecks. That's how content scales.

3
Execute

I lead creative teams through production, directing editors, videographers, and talent while staying close enough to the work to catch what needs to change before it becomes a problem.

4
Measure

I build custom dashboards that translate raw data (watch time, reach, engagement, retention) into clear creative decisions. Not just reporting numbers but telling leadership what they mean.

5
Optimize

I run content like a testing machine. Last month's performance is the baseline, not the ceiling. I test hooks, formats, and cadence, then scale what's proven and cut what's not.
